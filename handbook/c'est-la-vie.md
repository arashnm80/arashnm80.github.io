> C'est la vie. Leave or [fight](../fight) for the [narrow possibility](../narrow-way-through) to live.

Maybe it's what I want, maybe it's what I have to do, maybe it's my choice, maybe it's law of the universe or maybe it's sth written into my source code by my genes, family and society. Whatever it is, I know I can't tolerate to be just alive and it's the truth I can't run from

### Leave or Live
Half of me like this world, want to stay in it, want to feel and enjoy, want to experience, want to achieve, want to become better, want to listen to music, swim, sex, walk under rain.

And half of me have never wanted to be born, can't undersand this world, don't have any interest in this world, finds the costs and pain much further benefits and joy, just want to close my eyes, fall sleep and never wake up again.

In the end I have to choose one side.

### fight for the narrow possibility to live
First of all it's a fight. trying to live means to be in a constant fight; to fight with depression and lack of dopamine, to fight with loss of loved ones, to fight with pain and loneliness, to fight with tendency to give myself up to sadness and pressure, to fight with stress and anxiety, to fight with cruel acts of this cruel world, to fight with idiots, frauds and oppressives, and most importantly to fight agaist addiction to fight and try to see and seize very small opportunities to live because the reason to fight is to get rid of it.

Secondly it's a narrow possibility. It's a possibility because I can't be certain of anything in the world, I can just calculate, estimate and play with probabilities. And it's narrow because for my personality and the world I live in, it's actually very hard to find a path with positive Life Factor. I look around myself and I see at least 9 out of 10 that are only alive and are not living. This situation can be even harder for me too cause I'm not interested in this world as much as other humans and creatures.

### Life Factor
The ultimate metric to evaluate worthiness of life is LF: Integral of pleasure minus pain through lifetime

> In the end, it's not the years in my life that count. It's the life in my years.
